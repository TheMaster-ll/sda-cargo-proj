User Guide

Everything you need to manage shipments, track cargo, and handle billing within CargoPort.

Creating an Order

Orders are the core of CargoPort. Follow these steps to create a new shipment order from your dashboard.

1
Open the Orders page.
Click Orders in the left sidebar, then select the New Order button in the top-right corner.
2
Enter origin and destination.
Fill in the pickup address, delivery address, and preferred pickup date. CargoPort will auto-suggest addresses as you type.
3
Specify cargo details.
Enter the cargo weight (kg), dimensions, commodity type, and any special handling instructions such as fragile or temperature-controlled.
4
Select transport mode.
Choose Air, Road, or Sea. CargoPort will recommend a mode based on your cargo weight and urgency.
5
Review and submit.
Double-check all details on the summary screen, then click Submit Order. You will receive a confirmation email with your tracking number.
Tip: You can save partially completed orders as drafts and return to them later from the Drafts tab.

Tracking Shipments

CargoPort provides real-time tracking for all active shipments. There are two ways to track your cargo:

From the Dashboard

Your most recent shipments appear on the dashboard with live status indicators. Click any shipment card to open the detailed tracking view, which includes a map, estimated arrival time, and milestone timeline.

Using a Tracking Number

Enter your tracking number (format: CP-XXXXXXXX) in the search bar at the top of any page. The tracking detail page shows:

StatusMeaning
PendingOrder received and awaiting pickup
In TransitCargo is on the move to its destination
CustomsShipment is in customs clearance
DeliveredCargo has been delivered and signed for
Notifications: Enable push or email notifications in your profile settings to get real-time updates when your shipment status changes.

Invoice & Billing

CargoPort generates invoices automatically after each delivery. All billing information is accessible from the Billing section in the sidebar.

Viewing Invoices

Each invoice includes a breakdown of base freight charges, fuel surcharges, insurance (if selected), and applicable taxes. Click any invoice row to view or download a PDF copy.

Payment Methods

CargoPort supports credit/debit cards, bank transfers, and net-30 terms for verified business accounts. Manage your payment methods under Settings > Payment.

Disputing a Charge

If you notice a discrepancy, click the Dispute button on the invoice detail page. Our billing team will review your case within 2 business days.

Managing Your Profile

Keep your account information up to date by navigating to Settings > Profile.

Personal Information

Update your name, phone number, and company affiliation. Your email address serves as your login and can be changed under Account Security.

Notification Preferences

Choose which events trigger notifications and through which channels (email, SMS, or in-app). We recommend keeping shipment status alerts enabled at minimum.

Team Members

Business accounts can invite team members with specific roles: Admin (full access), Dispatcher (create and manage orders), or Viewer (read-only).
